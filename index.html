About the Fair

As seen on FOX NEWS, NCTV17, and Patch, the fair is run by local high school students and gives children ages 5-14 the opportunity to test the waters of entrepreneurship by starting their own businesses and selling products and services in a one-day, farmers market style event. Our past four fairs have welcomed over 500 kidpreneurs and 350 businesses, positively impacting an estimated 5000+ community members.

During the fair, participants showcase their businesses to the local community and receive feedback from both high school judges and local business professionals. Awards are given out at the end to standout businesses.

We also bring in guest speakers, from business professionals to past participants, to share their experiences and offer advice. Participation FAQ + Rules

Why should I participate?

First off - it's incredibly fun. You'll meet new friends and interact with experienced mentors along the way. Creating and running a business young helps kids gain hands-on skills for any career path.

How do I apply?

Applications must be submitted online. Submit one application for each business. Children working as a group should submit one application that includes each child’s information. No more than 3 participants per business.

If you need to back out after applying, please let us know at least 10 days in advance by contacting us.

What happens after I apply?

Applications are reviewed on a first come, first serve basis. We will get back to you within 5 business days. Once you're admitted, get started on your business and attend the three prefair workshops if you can.

Business ideas + competition

Two cupcake businesses? Not a problem. In general, we let market forces play out, though if we receive more than three similar businesses we will let later entries know so they can decide what to do.

What will be provided?

The fair is an indoor event. Each business will be provided with a 5' x 6' table and 1-3 chairs (depending on number of kidpreneurs). Participants will also receive a free kidpreneur bucket hat and a certificate of participation.

What will NOT be provided?

Electricity, water, and WiFi will not be provided.

Rules + expectations

This event is designed to give children the experience of selling a product or service. If a parent is found selling or promoting a child’s product or service, the child’s business will be disqualified.

Parents of younger children may sit in the booth, but the children should be responsible for set up, customer interaction, and sales. Parents may help their child fill out the application, but we expect the children to do as much as possible by themselves.

Product guidelines

We will only be accepting businesses selling original, handmade items. Unfortunately, this means resellers will not be able to participate.
